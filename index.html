Luis’s Padel Level Calculator

Padel is one of the fastest growing sports in the UK. In Cambridge, David Lloyd installed three outdoor Padel courts in 2024 and the club has a growing community of players. Most Padel players were new to racket sports or came from sports like tennis, squash, or badminton. This means that technical and tactical knowledge of the game is very heterogeneous. When arranging matches, the community sorts itself into three buckets: beginner, intermediate and advanced. It is also human nature that, when self-assessing, most players are overly optimistic about their levels. Managing Padel groups, I need more granular player scoring. Even player ratings are key to match arrangements. The games are more fun and competitive.


This is my first attempt at a self-scoring system and is work in progress; I welcome feedback. The system scores players between beginner and advanced levels, assigning numerical scores between 1 and 5. It is only appropriate for amateur and recreational players. In order to calculate your score, you have to answer all the questions below. You also have to be familiar with the terminology of the game.
